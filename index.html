Setup Guide

1

Download

Click Download for Mac above and unzip the file.

2

Open Clicker (first time only)

Option A: Right-click Clicker.app and select Open, then click Open again.

Option B: Double-click the app. If macOS says it can't be opened, click Done. Then go to System Settings → Privacy & Security, scroll down, and click Open Anyway.

3

Grant Accessibility Permission

Go to System Settings → Privacy & Security → Accessibility and toggle Clicker ON. This allows the app to simulate the Option key.

4

Scan the QR Code

Make sure your iPhone is on the same WiFi network as your Mac. Open the Camera app on your iPhone, point it at the QR code on your Mac, and tap the notification that appears.

5

Connect and Use

If the app doesn't auto-connect, enter the IP address shown on your Mac and tap Connect. Tap anywhere on screen to toggle the Option key on/off.

Perfect for:

Extended Option-key tasks, accessing hidden menu options, copying files instead of moving them, or any time you need to hold Option without touching your keyboard.

Troubleshooting

Option key not working?

Make sure Clicker is enabled in System Settings → Privacy & Security → Accessibility. You may need to remove and re-add it if it was previously denied.

Can't connect from iPhone?

Ensure both devices are on the same WiFi network. Try entering the IP address manually (shown on your Mac when Clicker is running).

No sound when tapping?

Make sure your iPhone isn't on silent mode. If using Chrome or Brave, the sound should work after the latest update.

To quit Clicker:

Open Activity Monitor, search for "Clicker", select it, and click the X button to stop it.

Requirements: Mac running macOS 10.15+, iPhone with iOS 15+, both on the same WiFi network.
